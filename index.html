Eclipse
by James Gallien

August 21, 2017

I'd seen a partial eclipse before so I wasn't especially concerned about the imminent total eclipse. The hype, though, was deafening. Folks traveling from across the country, maybe even farther, to little 'ol St. Louis for the big event. Glasses in hand, Allison and I, invited to a friend's rural property for the day, made the drive. Just like any old day of the week so far...

Then the glasses. Can't see anything through them. *Nothing*. Except the sun, if you look right at it. Eye protection on steriods. Just be sure not to walk into any walls...

At about 90% eclipse, the area fell under an evening-level lighting, the cicadas in the nearby tree began their evening sounds, and the outside security lighting turned on...

At totality, the moon's silhouette completely blocked the sun, allowing us to remove our glasses and see the sun's corona. Twilight-like darkness, stars visible, several astute observers pointing out Jupiter and Venus. A truly extraordinary astronomical event. One of the rare times the hype was right on the money. And we were lucky enough to get a cloudless day for it.
